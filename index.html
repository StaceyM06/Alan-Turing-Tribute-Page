Alan Turing

A mathematician, cryptologist who solved Enigma in 1941

This is an image from the documentary on Alan Turing, 'The Imitation Game'

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's Life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale , London

  • 1919 - Enrolled at St. Michael's School

  • 1926 - Enrolled at Sherborne School

  • 1935 - Elected a Fellow of King's College

  • 1936 - Published an article named "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"

  • 1938 - Earned his Ph.D. 19 from Princeton University; his manuscript introduces the notion of hypercomputation

  • Late 1938 - Joined the secret centre of the GC&CS at Bletchley Park

  • 1941 - It became possible to easily decipher Enigma code thanks to Turning's Enigma Machine

  • 1945 - Worked at the National Physical Laboratory

  • 1948 - Finished fourth in the Amateur Athletics Association Marathon

  • 1950 - Brought together science and philosophy. In the article "Computing Machinery and Intelligence"

  • 1950 - Published an article named, "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis"

  • 1951 - Charged with "manifest indecency and sexual perversion ", gross indecency according to the Criminal Law Amendment Act. For being openly homosexual.

  • 1952 - Excluded from the greatest scientific projects

  • 1953 - Finished the 'cure' (medication) for homosexuality

  • 1954 - Found dead. Autopsy says he died of cyanide poisoning
